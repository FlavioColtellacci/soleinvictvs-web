Philosophia · The Four Virtues

THE STOIC FOUNDATION

Sapientia Wisdom

Know what is in your control and what is not. Act decisively on the former. Release the latter completely. The man who knows himself knows everything.

Iustitia Justice

Treat every man as he deserves. Hold the standard. Do not compromise on virtue for convenience. Justice is not softness — it is precision.

Fortitudo Courage

Seek voluntary hardship. Deliberate difficulty. The man who chooses the hard path when he could choose the easy one — that man becomes unconquerable.

Temperantia Moderation

Nothing in excess. Discipline in all things. Eat simply. Sleep deliberately. Spend with intent. The man who masters himself has mastered the only kingdom that matters.
