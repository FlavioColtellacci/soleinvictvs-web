The Four Virtues

THE STOIC FOUNDATION

I Sapientia Wisdom

Know what is in your control. Act on it. Release what is not. This single practice dismantles most of what makes modern life unbearable.

II Iustitia Justice

Treat every person as they deserve — not as they expect. Say what is true. Do what is right. That is all justice asks.

III Fortitudo Courage

Fear is information, not instruction. Act in spite of it. The centurion's courage was not the absence of fear — it was the refusal to be governed by it.

IV Temperantia Moderation

Not abstinence. Not indulgence. Mastery. The man who controls himself controls everything worth controlling. Nothing rules him.
